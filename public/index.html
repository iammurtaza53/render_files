What Is Laser Hair Removal?
" Laser hair removal is a clinical treatment that uses a concentrated beam, the laser, to get rid of undesirable hair," discusses Dr. Rachel Westbay, a board-certified dermatologist at Marmur Medical in New York City. "The light is taken in by the pigment or melanin in the hair, which converts the light power into warm that harms the hair roots. This damage inhibits or postpones future hair development."


It's worth mentioning that while it's usually referred to as a "long-term" kind of hair removal, that's a little bit of a misnomer. Initially, several treatments are required, and maintenance treatments might be required, Westbay discusses. Pregnancy aside, she clarifies that not everybody is a terrific candidate for laser hair elimination, as well as it's most effective on those with lighter skin and also darker hair.


The Dangers of Laser Hair Elimination During Pregnancy
There are a few possible issues at play. Initially, there's the problem of what the laser could do to the unborn child. According to Hack, lasers release radiation, which can be in the form of light, ultraviolet, or infrared radiation, says Hack.


She includes that they have differing levels of threat courses, and the extra powerful the laser, the greater the course. "Effective lasers can present a threat if misused. Radiation poses a danger to the developing fetus, specifically as the body organs are creating in the first trimester. Direct exposure to high doses of radiation can cause losing the unborn baby, preterm birth, and fetal malformations," she claims.


Westbay points out that laser hair removal, particularly, might not be troublesome. "There is no reason to presume that there can be any sensible damage to an unborn child from the energy sources," she says. "Optional laser therapies are generally not advised while pregnant, however no evidence sustains this. The density of the pregnant abdominal area, womb, and also amniotic liquid make it not likely that considerable amounts of laser power would certainly reach the unborn child throughout cutaneous laser therapy."


Westbay points out a 2019 review of 22 researches that considered 380 females in all trimesters of maternity that were treated with different laser wavelengths. There was only one clinically-significant event, a situation of early membrane layer tear without any additional morbidity.1 She discusses that the cause had not been clear; it doubted whether it was associated with the laser procedure.


Nevertheless, there are still various other possible threats of lasers that are more particular. "A few of the damaging side effects of laser treatments include shed skin and hyperpigmentation, which can be more probable to happen in pregnant women ," clarifies Shelley D'Aquino, owner of as well as head esthetician at Le Parlour NYC Laser Medspa. Hormone modifications increase the chance of skin dimming as well as melasma, which boosts the possibility for these dangers, she claims. The laser can not tell the difference between pigmented hairs and skin, adds Westbay.


Additionally, expectant individuals have an endangered body immune system, as well as any burn can take longer to heal, leading to infection and also potentially even marking, Westbay notes. Finally, not all topical anesthetics-- typically made use of during a laser hair removal treatment-- are thought about risk-free in maternity: "For example, lidocaine and also prilocaine are taken into consideration maternity category B, yet tetracaine is not," Westbay clarifies.


Alternative Pregnancy-Safe Hair Removal Techniques
Fortunately: You have plenty of other choices. "There are many alternatives to laser hair elimination for doing away with undesirable hair while expecting. You can wax, thread, tweeze or cut," states D'Aquino.


Westbay includes sugaring to that listing, although she keeps in mind staying clear of depilatory creams is possibly much better. "The science is still out regarding whether they're secure while pregnant. They contain the active ingredients barium sulfide powder and also calcium thioglycolate, which trigger the hair to liquify right into a jelly-like mass that can be wiped far from the skin. Although no proof shows depilatory lotions harm establishing babies, skipping them is probably best. Plus, because their odor is so pungent, they can likely exacerbate any type of nausea in an anticipating mama," she says.


The Bottom Line

While there's still really little data or research studies on the risks of laser hair elimination while pregnant, it's never ever a poor concept to err on the side of caution and also opt for other hair elimination choices till you deliver. That being claimed, if you do really feel highly that you want to think about hair removal while pregnant, make certain to discuss the dangers with your physician ahead of time, she includes.
