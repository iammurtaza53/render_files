What Is Laser Hair Removal?
" Laser hair removal is a medical treatment that uses a concentrated beam, the laser, to eliminate undesirable hair," discusses Dr. Rachel Westbay, a board-certified dermatologist at Marmur Medical in New York City City. "The light is absorbed by the pigment or melanin in the hair, which converts the light energy into heat that damages the hair follicles. This damages prevents or delays future hair growth."


It deserves stating that while it's usually referred to as a "permanent" type of hair removal, that's a little bit of a misnomer. Initially, numerous treatments are needed, as well as maintenance treatments may be needed, Westbay describes. Pregnancy aside, she clarifies that not every person is an excellent prospect for laser hair removal, as well as it's most reliable on those with lighter skin as well as darker hair.


The Risks of Laser Hair Elimination During Pregnancy
There are a few possible problems at play. First, there's the concern of what the laser might do to the unborn child. According to Hack, lasers emit radiation, which can be in the form of light, ultraviolet, or infrared radiation, claims Hack.


She includes that they have differing degrees of risk classes, as well as the a lot more powerful the laser, the higher the course. "Powerful lasers can posture a threat if mistreated. Radiation postures a danger to the creating fetus, especially as the organs are creating in the first trimester. Exposure to high dosages of radiation can result in miscarriage, preterm birth, and fetal malformations," she states.


Westbay explains that laser hair elimination, specifically, might not be troublesome. "There is no reason to presume that there can be any practical damages to a fetus from the power resources," she claims. "Elective laser treatments are typically not recommended while pregnant, yet no evidence supports this. The density of the pregnant abdominal area, womb, and also amniotic liquid make it unlikely that significant quantities of laser energy would certainly reach the unborn child throughout cutaneous laser treatment."


Westbay cites a 2019 testimonial of 22 studies that took a look at 380 ladies in all trimesters of maternity that were treated with different laser wavelengths. There was just one clinically-significant occasion, a case of early membrane layer rupture without any further morbidity.1 She clarifies that the reason wasn't clear; it doubted whether it was related to the laser procedure.


Nevertheless, there are still other possible dangers of lasers that are a lot more particular. "Some of the adverse effects of laser treatments consist of shed skin and hyperpigmentation, which can be more likely to happen in pregnant women ," clarifies Shelley D'Aquino, owner of and head esthetician at Le Parlour New York City Laser Health Club. Hormone changes raise the possibility of skin darkening and also melasma, which boosts the potential for these threats, she states. The laser can not inform the distinction between pigmented hairs and also skin, adds Westbay.


In addition, pregnant individuals have a compromised immune system, as well as any burn can take longer to heal, causing infection and also possibly even marking, Westbay notes. Finally, not all topical anesthetics-- typically made use of throughout a laser hair removal treatment-- are taken into consideration risk-free in pregnancy: "For example, lidocaine as well as prilocaine are taken into consideration maternity category B, yet tetracaine is not," Westbay clarifies.


Alternative Pregnancy-Safe Hair Removal Techniques
Fortunately: You have lots of other alternatives. "There are many options to laser hair removal for eliminating unwanted hair while pregnant. You can wax, string, tweeze or cut," says D'Aquino.


Westbay includes sugaring to that checklist, although she notes preventing depilatory lotions is probably better. "The scientific research is still out regarding whether they're safe during pregnancy. They contain the energetic components barium sulfide powder as well as calcium thioglycolate, which create the hair to liquify right into a jelly-like mass that can be wiped away from the skin. Although no evidence shows depilatory lotions harm developing infants, missing them is possibly best. And also, because their smell is so pungent, they can likely intensify any type of queasiness in an expecting mom," she says.


The Bottom Line

While there's still really little information or research studies on the risks of laser hair elimination while pregnant, it's never a negative concept to err on the side of caution and select other hair elimination choices until you deliver. That being stated, if you do feel highly that you want to consider hair removal while expecting, make certain to discuss the dangers with your doctor in advance, she adds.
